LET BOARD PASSERS

The Philippine College of Northwestern Luzon (PCNL) proudly celebrates the remarkable achievement of its graduates who successfully passed the Licensure Examination for Teachers (LET). These future educators have proven that with dedication, perseverance, and passion, dreams truly do come true. The journey to becoming a licensed teacher is filled with challenges—countless lesson plans, long review hours, and endless moments of preparation. But through it all, these PCNLians stood strong, driven by their desire to educate, inspire, and make a difference. Passing the LET is more than just a personal success; it is a commitment to serve the next generation with knowledge, wisdom, and compassion. Our board passers now carry the responsibility of shaping young minds and contributing to the betterment of our society through quality education.

CRIMINOLOGY BOARD PASSERS

The Philippine College of Northwestern Luzon (PCNL) is once again beaming with pride as we celebrate the success of our very own Criminology graduates who have successfully passed the Criminology Licensure Examination. This achievement marks a major milestone—not only in their personal journey, but also in the legacy of excellence upheld by PCNL. With dedication, sacrifice, and the guidance of our committed faculty members, these newly licensed criminologists have proven that hard work truly pays off. Their names now stand as a testament to the quality education and strong values that PCNL instills in its students.


You are not just board passers—you are future protectors of justice, and you make the entire PCNL community proud. Mabuhay kayong lahat, bagong Criminologists ng PCNL!

Tuguegarao City, Cagayan – April 11, 2025 – Student-athlete Flores, also known by her first name Loren, From Philippine College of Northwestern Luzon(PCNL), achieved an impressive 6th place finish at the 2025 Private Schools Athletic Association (PRISAA) National Games held in Tuguegarao, Cagayan. Competing in the 3-kilometer walkathon, Loren Flores showcased remarkable endurance, technique, and determination. Battling the intense heat and stiff competition, she stood out for her strong pacing and disciplined form, which earned her a spot among the top finishers in the event. Flores’ performance is the result of months of rigorous training and a deep commitment to her sport. Under the guidance of her coach and with the support of her school, Philippine College of Northwestern Luzon (PCNL), she was able to elevate her skills and represent her institution with pride on the national stage. The PRISAA Games is an annual multi-sport event that gathers top athletes from private schools across the country. It serves as a platform for showcasing young talent and fostering sportsmanship, discipline, and unity. Loren Flores’ 6th place finish is not just a personal victory—it’s also a proud moment for her school and community, inspiring fellow students and aspiring athletes to pursue excellence in both academics and sports.
